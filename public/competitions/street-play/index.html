NUKKAD NATAK

The street play translated in Hindi as “Nukkad Natak”, is an attempt to give students a platform to enact existing or foreseen environmental problems in a way they see fit to make other people aware about it.

Register

About Event

Introduction:

Street theatre is a form of theatrical performance and presentation in outdoor public spaces without a specific paying audience. Artists with an interest in social activism may choose to stage their work on the street as a means of directly confronting or engaging the public. Promoting environmental awareness is an easy way to become an environmental steward and participate in creating a brighter future for our children.
Street plays allow participants to:
  • Analysing issues deeply, thinking about the cause, effect and solution of it
  • Develop their creative acting skills, expressiveness and coordination
  • Boost their confidence
  • Engage with the community

Theme:

Any current environmental issue can be chosen by the teams to generate sensitivity and make public more aware of the issue and how important it is to take the necessary steps to curb it.

Event Guidelines:

  • Teams should pick a topic by 18th April 2018 and inform the organizers about the topic prior to the day of the event
  • Every team will be given 1 min to prepare and organize starting scene.
  • The time duration of the play will be 7-10min
  • Adhere to the time limit.
  • Props required during the performance must be the responsibility of the participants
  • Any extra material required in the form of placards and slogans will be made by participants
  • The medium of language: a mix of English and Hindi both can be used.

Registration details

  • Participate in TEAMS of 5-8 people each
  • Total no. of teams: 6
  • Class- 7-9th
  • Total no. of participants:48

Judgment Criteria

  • Expression
  • Audience engagement
  • Time limit
  • Creativity in presentation
  • Team coordination

Why Join

To Promote the importance of Green Energy for a sustainable environment.

To bring environmental education (EE) out of the classrooms

To engage students and make it fun for them

To encourage students to think about current environmental problems and possible solutions

To provide a relaxed environment for children to interact and learn from their peers
